General Summary

ZAE#201-G, designated as "Glacien", is an anthropomorphic entity classified as protogen, exhibiting lupine characteristics. It has an approximate height of 1.75 meters, a total weight of 91kg without wings, and appears to be 20 years old. Its fur is predominantly white with dark fur around its neck. The entity presents wing appendages with a translucent crystalline structure and an ice crown formation that maintains its solid state even at temperatures exceeding 60°C.

Despite an affable appearance and generally cooperative behavior, it demonstrates anomalous abilities intrinsically linked to the manipulation of cryogenic phenomena and cold weather conditions. These capabilities include localized emission of snow particles with thermoregulatory effects for allies and immobilizing properties for hostile targets. Additionally, it can generate high-density ice formations, such as stalactites and spikes, with offensive potential. Its ability to induce a controlled decrease in ambient temperature within an approximate radius of 15 meters has been documented. Aerial locomotion is possible through the use of its wings, allowing stable displacement albeit at limited speed.

Entity ZAE#201-G exhibits above-average intelligence. However, its communication pattern and conduct have been described by staff as "surprisingly atypical". It manifests recurring use of informal language and expressions lacking apparent logic in inappropriate contexts, although it has demonstrated thorough knowledge and compliance with security protocols and operational standards. The current working hypothesis suggests that this behavior is deliberate and aims to foster an emotionally positive environment within the facilities.

It wears a textile badge on its upper left extremity, which represents the gay pride flag. At the express request of the entity, this badge must not be removed under any circumstances or containment protocol, as it constitutes a fundamental element of its identity. Previous removal attempts have caused severe mood alterations in the subject, accompanied by anomalous manifestations of extreme weather.
